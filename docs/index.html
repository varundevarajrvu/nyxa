say it

Speak plainly. Every command passes a gate.

Say “hey jarvis” — or clap three times — wait for the orb to brighten, then ask. Each action carries a permission tier that decides how much ceremony stands between your words and the machine.

  • open youtube and search lo-fi study beats SAFE-AUTO reversible — runs the moment you finish speaking
  • play believer on spotify SAFE-AUTO finds and presses play in the Spotify app itself — no API key
  • close the youtube tab NEEDS-CONFIRM Nyxa asks “yes or no?” out loud and waits for your answer
  • delete report.pdf DENY-BY-DEFAULT pops a dialog you must physically click — a voice can’t approve it, and files go to the Recycle Bin, never gone for good

how it hears

From breath to deed, without leaving the room.

  1. Wake word openWakeWord · ~5% of one core

    A two-second ring buffer, read by one scorer, waiting for “hey jarvis”. Below the threshold, audio is simply overwritten.

  2. Capture Silero VAD

    The orb brightens, your sentence is taken whole, and the endpointer knows when you've finished.

  3. Transcription Parakeet TDT 0.6B · on-device

    Speech becomes text in 150–350 ms, on your CPU.

  4. Intent grammar first · local LLM fallback

    A grammar over the action registry resolves most commands instantly with no model at all. What it misses goes to a local LLM via Ollama — schema-validated, and refused on any doubt.

  5. The gate tier comes from the registry, never from a model

    No model output can name, change, or downgrade a permission tier. Any error, anywhere, and nothing executes.

Before the wake word fires, nothing is recorded, transcribed, or stored — and with a local LLM, nothing you say ever leaves the machine. Every outcome, including refusals, is written to an append-only audit log you can read.

the gates

Three tiers between a voice and a side effect.

SAFE-AUTO

Runs at once

Read-only or trivially reversible: open, search, play, pause, volume.

NEEDS-CONFIRM

Asks aloud

State-changing but recoverable: closing apps and tabs, moving files. A spoken “yes” proceeds; anything else — including silence — cancels.

DENY-BY-DEFAULT

Demands a hand

Destructive or outward-facing: deleting, sending, installing. Approval is a physical click on a dialog, so a stray voice — yours or your TV's — cannot grant it.

And when you want silence: Ctrl+Shift+Alt+K halts everything, instantly, from any state. Resume is manual — a kill is never quietly self-healed.

hands

It watches for gestures the way it listens for words.

Turn on gesture control and the orb follows your hand through the webcam — one hand to pan, two hands spread apart to zoom. Hand tracking runs locally too (MediaPipe); the camera feed never leaves the page, let alone the machine. Try it above: the orb already leans toward your pointer.

summoning

Four commands and it lives on your machine.

# requires Rust and a mic; Ollama is optional but recommended
> git clone https://github.com/varundevarajrvu/nyxa && cd nyxa
> powershell -ExecutionPolicy Bypass -File scripts\fetch_models.ps1
> cargo build --release -p jarvis-app
> .\target\release\jarvis-app.exe

Full setup, configuration, and the action registry are in the README.
